Solunar Trail User Manual

Last updated: June 13, 2026

Solunar Trail is an outdoor companion for solar and lunar data, solunar activity periods, saved places, GPS status, compass/orientation tools, and activity tracking.

Getting Started

  1. Open the app and grant location permission when prompted.
  2. Use the bottom tabs to switch between solar/lunar data, saved locations, GPS/orientation tools, tracking, settings, and app information.
  3. For best GPS results, use the app outdoors with a clear view of the sky.
Safety note: Solunar Trail is informational only. Do not rely on it as your only source for navigation, weather, emergency communication, or safety-critical decisions.

Solar & Lunar

The Solar & Lunar page shows sun and moon information for your current location or a saved location, including solunar activity periods.

  • Major periods are based on moon overhead and underfoot timing.
  • Minor periods are based on moonrise and moonset timing.
  • Dawn/dusk overlap may indicate a stronger activity window.
  • The star rating summarizes the day's expected solunar activity.

Locations

Use saved locations for repeat outdoor spots such as trailheads, overlooks, stands, camps, or fishing areas.

  • Save your current position for later use.
  • Rename or delete saved places as needed.
  • Select a saved location to view solar, lunar, and solunar information for that spot.

GPS Status and Orientation

The GPS and orientation tools help you understand what your device is seeing.

  • GPS status shows live fix details such as coordinates, altitude, accuracy, and movement data when available.
  • Orientation shows compass heading and device sensor readings.
  • Compass accuracy depends on your device sensors and nearby magnetic interference. Calibrate or move away from metal/electronics if the heading looks wrong.

Tracking an Activity

The Tracking page records your route and activity stats locally on your device.

  1. Choose the tracking mode in Settings if needed.
  2. Tap Start to begin recording.
  3. Use Pause and Resume to temporarily stop/resume the timer and GPS collection.
  4. Tap Stop to save the track.

Lap / Segment Markers

In General tracking mode, tap Lap to drop a segment boundary during a recording.

  • The Tracking page shows a live Segments count after the first lap.
  • Per-segment stats include distance, elevation gain, duration, moving time, and average moving speed.
  • If you tap Lap again before a new GPS point is captured, the app asks you to move a bit before the next lap instead of creating an empty segment.

Saved Track Details

Saved tracks include route stats such as distance, duration, moving time, elevation, speed, and GPS point count. Tracks with segment markers show a per-segment breakdown in the detail view.

Editing Track Segments

Saved tracks can be edited to add, remove, or clear segment markers. Segment markers divide a track into meaningful sections without changing the original recorded route.

  • Use the slider to choose a point along the track.
  • Add a marker at the selected point.
  • Remove the nearest marker or clear all markers if needed.
  • Markers are exported as GPX waypoints when exporting a track.

Maps and GPX Export

Map views use Google Maps to display locations and tracks. Saved tracks can be exported as GPX files for use in other mapping or GPS tools.

Settings

Settings include preferences such as distance units, theme, map type, GPS display mode, north reference, and tracking mode.

Privacy and Data

Solunar Trail stores saved locations, preferences, and recorded tracks locally on your device. It does not include analytics, advertising, or crash reporting SDKs. See the Solunar Trail Privacy Policy for details.

Troubleshooting

  • No GPS fix: go outdoors, enable device location, and wait for a clear fix.
  • Tracking looks stationary: your device may not have received a new GPS point yet; move a short distance and wait for the next fix.
  • Compass seems wrong: move away from metal objects/electronics and recalibrate the device sensors.
  • Map does not load: check network connectivity. Google Maps tiles require network access.
